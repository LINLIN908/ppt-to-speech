# Thesis Defense Scenario

For PhD/Master's thesis and proposal defenses, and comprehensive exams. The audience is a committee whose job is to probe; the speaker's job is to demonstrate command of the work, not to sell it.

**Theoretical grounding.** Script the defense as an argument, not a summary: Toulmin's model identifies six elements in any argument — claim (your conclusion), data (your evidence), warrant (the inferential link from data to claim), backing (the basis for the warrant itself), qualifier (the word that limits how strongly you assert the claim), and rebuttal (the conditions under which the claim doesn't hold). Committee questions work in layers: first they attack warrants ("why does that data support that conclusion?"), then they attack backing ("and what makes that reasoning itself valid?"). The script should make key warrants explicit rather than leave them implied, and should be prepared to defend the backing one level deeper. Qualifier maps directly onto Hyland's hedging research: credibility comes from one well-chosen qualifier per claim — the Toulmin element and Hyland's finding are describing the same thing from different angles. Structure the motivation section as a spoken CARS move (Swales): establish the territory, carve the niche, occupy it.

## Tone

- Measured, precise, first-person but modest: "we found", "our results suggest" — never "this proves" or "groundbreaking".
- Hedge claims honestly but don't drown in hedges. One qualifier per claim: "Among the 191 studies we coded, topic prevalence shifted toward LLMs after 2022" — not "it might possibly seem to perhaps suggest".
- Defensive soundness over excitement. Every causal verb ("leads to", "improves") will be challenged; only use them where the design supports them, otherwise say "is associated with".

## Structural conventions

A defense talk almost always follows the deck, which almost always follows the document: motivation → research questions → related work → method → results → discussion → limitations → contributions. The script should exploit this:

- **Opening (30–45s):** Greet the committee by role ("Dear committee members, Professor X, ..."), state your name and the title, give a one-sentence statement of the contribution. Do not start with an anecdote or a joke unless the user asks; defenses reward directness.
- **Research questions slide:** Read the RQs verbatim and slowly — this is the one slide where reading the slide aloud is correct, because everything after is judged against these exact words. Flag this in delivery cues.
- **Method slides:** Highest precision zone. Name the sample size, instruments, and analysis choices out loud even if they're on the slide, because committee members compose their questions here.
- **Limitations slide:** Never rush it. Owning limitations preempts the harshest questions. The script should present each limitation paired with its mitigation: "X is a limitation; we partially address it by Y; fully resolving it is future work."
- **Closing:** Restate contributions in one breath each, thank the committee, and explicitly invite questions: "I welcome your questions and feedback."

## Q&A specifics for defenses

- The graceful "I don't know": "That's an important question I haven't examined directly. My intuition based on [adjacent finding] is..., but I would need to [concrete step] to answer it properly." Committees reward epistemic honesty and punish bluffing — say so in the prep notes.
- Expert questions to always prepare: why this method over the obvious alternative; how a key construct was operationalized; what happens to the findings if a core assumption fails; how the work differs from the closest prior work (name it).
- Novice-style questions still occur (a committee member outside the subfield): prepare plain-language definitions of the 3–4 most load-bearing technical terms in the deck.
- Skeptic question: find the inferential leap between results and claimed contribution — that gap is where the hardest question lives.

## Language notes

- Chinese-language defenses (答辩) open with「各位评委老师，大家好」and close with「我的汇报到此结束，恳请各位老师批评指正」. Use these conventional frames; they are expected, not clichés.
- For Chinese delivery of English slides, keep instrument names, model names, and statistics in English as written on the slide; speak the connective tissue in Chinese.
