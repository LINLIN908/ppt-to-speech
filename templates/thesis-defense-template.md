# Thesis Defense — Script Template

Opening/closing frames, time distribution, and structural patterns for thesis and proposal defenses. script_writer_agent reads this alongside `references/academic/thesis-defense.md`.

---

## Opening frames

**English (final defense):**
> "Dear committee members — Professor [X], Professor [Y], and [remaining members]. My name is [Name]. I'm here today to defend my [dissertation / thesis] titled '[Title]'. In short, this work [one-sentence contribution in plain language]."

**English (proposal defense):**
> "Dear committee members. My name is [Name]. Today I'm proposing a study of [topic]. The central question I aim to answer is [RQ in one sentence]. I'll walk you through my rationale, design, and timeline, and I look forward to your guidance."

**Chinese (答辩 / 论文答辩):**
> 「各位评委老师，大家好。我叫[姓名]，今天我汇报的论文题目是《[题目]》，指导老师是[导师姓名]老师。我的研究[一句话贡献陈述]。下面请各位老师指导。」

**Chinese (开题报告):**
> 「各位评委老师，大家好。我叫[姓名]，今天向大家汇报我的开题报告，题目是《[题目]》。[一句话研究问题]。以下是我的研究设计，请各位老师批评指正。」

---

## Structural patterns

**Research questions slide** — this is the one slide where reading the slide aloud is correct. Slow down:
> `[read verbatim and slowly — committee measures everything that follows against these exact words]`

**Method slides** — name sample size, instruments, and key choices out loud even if visible on slide:
> "[Instrument name] was administered to [N] participants over [period]. We chose [X] over [Y] because [one-sentence rationale]."

**Limitations slide** — never rush; pair each limitation with its mitigation:
> "X is a limitation of this study. We partially address it by [Y]; fully resolving it is outside the scope of this work and is a direction for future research."

**Results interpretation** — hedge claims precisely; avoid causal verbs unless the design supports them:
> "Among the [N] [units], we found that [pattern]. This is consistent with [implication], though [causal verb] would require [stronger design]."

**Contributions restatement** — one breath per contribution, before closing:
> "To summarize: this work contributes [1], [2], and [3]."

---

## Closing frames

**English:**
> "That completes my presentation. To summarize my contributions: [C1], [C2], [C3]. I welcome your questions and feedback."

**Chinese:**
> 「以上是我的全部汇报内容。我的主要贡献是：[C1]、[C2]。[如有时间]具体来说……我的汇报到此结束，恳请各位老师批评指正。」

## Time distribution (reference only — adjust to deck structure)

| Section | % of total time | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Opening + motivation | 10–15% | Never more than 15%; committees want to get to the substance |
| Related work | 5–10% | Enough to show awareness; not a literature tour |
| Method | 20–25% | High-precision zone; committees compose questions here |
| Results + discussion | 30–40% | Heart of the defense |
| Limitations | 8–12% | Never rush; owning limitations preempts the harshest questions |
| Contributions + closing | 5–8% | Brief but complete |

---

## Transition patterns

**Into method:**
> "With that motivation established, let me walk you through how we designed the study."

**From method into results:**
> "Having described the design, I want to show you what we found."

**From results into discussion:**
> "These patterns in the data point to a few key implications I want to unpack."

**From discussion into limitations:**
> "Before I turn to contributions, I want to be direct about the limitations of this work."

**From limitations into contributions:**
> "Despite these constraints, I believe this study makes several contributions."

## Delivery cues for defenses

- `[read verbatim — RQs slide]` — exactly once, on the research questions slide
- `[slow down — sample size / instrument name]` — on every method choice that could be challenged
- `[pause]` — after stating each contribution; let it land before moving to the next
- `[invite: pause for follow-up]` — after limitations, if time allows; committees appreciate the space
