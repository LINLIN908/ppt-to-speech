# Conference Talk — Script Template

Opening hooks, takeaway construction, time distribution, and structural patterns for 10–15 minute conference paper presentations. script_writer_agent reads this alongside `references/academic/conference-talk.md`.

---

## The takeaway sentence — write this first

Before writing any other part of the script, draft the takeaway: the one sentence the audience can repeat at dinner. This is the talk's thesis. Everything else exists to earn it.

> "We show that [finding], which means [implication for the field]."  
> "Contrary to conventional wisdom, [result] — and here is why it matters."  
> "[Method/construct] predicts [outcome] better than [previous best], even when [condition]."

---

## Opening hooks — choose one

**The puzzle:**
> "[Common assumption the field holds]. But in the [N] studies we analyzed / the data we collected, [surprising counter-pattern]. I'm [Name] from [Institution], and today I want to show you why [puzzle matters and what this paper does about it]."

**The surprising number (from your own results):**
> "[Striking number from your key finding]. That's the result we found. I'm [Name], and I want to walk you through how we got there and what it means for [subfield]."

**The gap as provocation:**
> "We've spent [X years / the last decade] studying [X]. Almost no one has studied [Y]. [Name], [Institution]. This talk is about closing that gap — and what we found surprised us."

**Do NOT open with:**
- "Good morning everyone, my name is..."
- "First I will talk about..., then..."
- Narrating the outline slide

---

## Structural patterns

**Related work (30 seconds maximum):**
> "This work builds directly on [Citation A], which showed [finding], and [Citation B], which identified [gap]. What's missing is [your contribution]. That's what we add."

**Method sketch:**
> "[Design] with [N] [units] over [period]. The key analysis is [approach]. The details are in the paper — I want to spend our time on the results."

`[backup slide note: Slide X addresses the question about [robustness check]; I'll go there if asked]`

**Walking a figure:**
> "Each [dot/bar/line] here represents [unit]. The [x-axis] shows [variable]; [y-axis] shows [outcome]. The gap between [A] and [B] — that's the effect."

**The "it's in the paper" move** — legitimate and encouraged for conference talks:
> "The full robustness analysis is in Section [N] — happy to discuss it in the Q&A."

---

## Closing frames

**English:**
> "[Takeaway sentence]. The paper is on [arXiv / in the proceedings] if you want the details. I'd love to talk more — [find me at the poster session / here's my card / come say hi after]."

**Chinese domestic conference (学术年会):**
> 「[Takeaway sentence]。论文在[会议论文集/预印本]中。欢迎会后交流，谢谢大家。」

## Time distribution

| Section | % of total time | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Hook + framing | 5–8% | 20–30s max; hook first, then name/affiliation |
| Related work | 3–5% | 30s max; "what's missing," not a tour |
| Method | 12–18% | Sketch only; move robustness to backup slides |
| Results | 40–50% | Heart of the talk; one figure walked slowly > four figures rushed |
| Discussion + takeaway | 8–12% | One idea, said confidently |
| Closing | 3–5% | Invitation, not conclusion |
| Buffer for transitions | 8–10% | |

For a 12-minute slot: roughly 1 minute opening, 30s related work, 2 minutes method, 5–6 minutes results, 1.5 minutes discussion/takeaway, 30s closing.

---

## Transition patterns

**Hook into framing:**
> "That puzzle is what this paper addresses."

**Framing into method:**
> "To answer that question, we [brief design summary]."

**Method into results:**
> "Let me show you what we found."

**Result 1 into result 2:**
> "But the effect isn't uniform. When we break the sample by [variable]..."

**Results into takeaway:**
> "What does this add up to?"

---

## Delivery cues for conference talks

- `[energetic — hook must land before name/title]` — first 15 seconds only
- `[slow down — key figure: explain axes before the finding]` — on every result figure
- `[point to gap in chart]` — when the visual makes the effect visible
- `[it's in the paper]` — on any robustness or detail slide; say it confidently, not apologetically
- `[backup: Slide X]` — annotate backup slides in the script so the presenter knows which question each answers
