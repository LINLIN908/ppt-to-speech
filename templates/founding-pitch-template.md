# Founding Pitch — Script Template

Hook structures, problem/solution/ask patterns, and time distribution for investor pitches, demo days, and startup competitions. script_writer_agent reads this alongside `references/founding-pitch.md`.

---

## The spoken arc — always

Hook → problem → solution → proof → ask

The deck structure may vary; the spoken arc does not. If the deck doesn't follow this order, the script imposes it through transitions.

---

## Opening hooks (15–20 seconds) — choose one

**Startling number:**
> "[Number that makes the problem visceral and expensive]. [One-sentence translation of what that means]. [Company name] is how [specific persona] [specific solution verb, plain language]."

Example: "Every year, $4.3 billion is spent on employee onboarding that doesn't stick — because training happens once and then stops. Meridian makes training continuous."

**One-sentence customer story:**
> "[Name/role] spent [time or money] on [painful task]. Last [period], [company name] cut that to [better outcome]. [Brief statement of how]."

**Contrarian belief:**
> "Everyone in [industry] assumes [conventional wisdom]. We built [company] on the opposite bet: [belief]. And it's working."

**After the hook — name and company:**
> "I'm [Name], [Company]. [One sentence: what the product does, for whom, in plain words a smart 12-year-old follows]."

**Never open with:** "Hi, today I'll present [company name]..." or any variant of announcing that you are about to present.

---

## Problem slide patterns

Answer two questions in the script, even if they're on the slide: **who bleeds**, and **how much does the bleeding cost**.

> "[Persona] faces [pain point]. Today they [current bad solution — the workaround]. That costs them [specific impact: time, money, or outcome]. And it's getting worse because [trend]."

**Avoid:** abstract market framing ("the X industry faces significant challenges"). Make it personal and expensive.

---

## Solution/demo slide patterns

Shortest sentences in the whole script — one capability per breath:

> "[Company name] is [what it is, in plain words]. Here's what it does: [capability 1]. [capability 2]. [capability 3]. That's it."

**Demo recovery line** (always have this ready):
> "While that loads — the result you're about to see is [the outcome]. The core of what I want you to take away is [one capability]."

---

## Traction slide — slow down

This is the equivalent of the defense's method slide — investors form their questions here. Speak every number and its time window precisely:

> "Since [specific date], we've reached [metric with units]. That's [growth rate] [timeframe]. Our [key leading indicator — MAU / ARR / NPS] is [number]."

`[slow down — traction slide; investors are composing questions here]`

---

## Team slide formula

One sentence per person. Formula: **unfair advantage**, not biography.

> "[Name] [specific credential that makes them the right person for this specific problem]."

Good: "Lin built the curriculum used in 200 schools across Shandong province."  
Bad: "Lin has a PhD in Education from [University] and 8 years of experience."

---

## The ask

Many founders mumble the ask. The script must make it explicit and mark it:

> `[slow down — the ask]` "We're raising [specific amount] to [specific milestone] in [timeframe]. That gets us to [next inflection point — the event that changes the company's trajectory]."

State the number out loud, clearly, and stop after it. Do not hedge ("we're thinking about raising around maybe...").

---

## Closing

One sentence of vision, then stop:

> "[Vision sentence in one breath — the world as it is if the company succeeds]. [Pause.] Thank you."

No "thank you for your time" slide narration. No trailing off. End on the vision.

---

## Time distribution

| Section | % of total time | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Hook + intro | 5–8% | 15–20s; hook first, name second |
| Problem | 10–15% | Personal and expensive; not abstract market framing |
| Solution / demo | 15–20% | Short sentences; one capability per breath |
| Traction | 15–20% | Slow down; speak every number precisely |
| Market size | 5–10% | Bottom-up math spoken; skip top-down TAM theater |
| Business model | 5–10% | Who pays, how much, what the unit looks like |
| Team | 8–12% | Unfair advantage, not biography |
| The ask | 5–8% | `[slow down]`; number + milestone + timeframe |
| Closing vision | 2–3% | One sentence, full stop |

## Chinese pitch (路演) notes

Hook-first rule still applies — do not open with「大家好，我来自……」before the hook.

Avoid Silicon Valley jargon translated into Chinese: 「赋能」「抓手」「闭环」「颗粒度」「生态」(as metaphor). Use the plain verb instead:
- 「赋能客户」→ 「帮客户做到X」
- 「打通闭环」→ 「让X和Y连起来」

Keep brand names, metrics (MAU, ARR, CAC, LTV), and product terms in English even in Chinese delivery — that is how the room talks.

---

## Delivery cues for pitches

- `[slow down — the ask]` — mandatory on the ask slide
- `[slow down — traction]` — mandatory on the traction slide
- `[demo recovery ready]` — annotate the demo slide; have the recovery line memorized
- `[confident declarative — no hedging]` — on every claim slide; investors discount hedged claims to zero
- `[one sentence per person]` — on team slide; do not expand
