# Panel Discussion — Script Template

Opening statement structure, talking point frames, reaction material patterns, and timing guidance for panels and roundtables. script_writer_agent reads this alongside `references/academic/panel-discussion.md`.

---

## Critical difference from other scenarios

This scenario does **not** produce a per-slide script. The deliverable is a **position kit**: material to be internalized, not read. Every section should be written so the presenter can rehearse it until it compresses into recall cues.

Tell the user at the top of the output: "This is material to internalize for recall, not to read aloud. Read each section until you can say it in your own words, without looking."

---

## Opening statement (2–5 min, confirm allotted time)

**Structure:** stance → evidence → implication that invites discussion.

**Template:**
> "[Clear, committable position on the panel theme.] I draw this from [strongest piece of evidence or specific experience — one or two concrete sentences]. What that means for today's conversation is [implication or question that invites others to engage]."

**Examples by domain:**

*Education research:*
> "Teacher confidence matters more than technology access when it comes to EdTech adoption. In the 40 schools in our study, infrastructure wasn't the binding constraint in a single case — professional development was. So when we talk about scaling EdTech, the question I'd put to this panel is whether we're solving the right bottleneck."

*Policy:*
> "Regulation is lagging AI deployment by three to five years in most jurisdictions — and that gap is getting wider, not narrower. I base that on [source/experience]. What I'd like us to examine today is whether voluntary frameworks can actually close that gap, or whether we're rationalizing inaction."

**Stance requirements:**
- Must be specific enough that a reasonable colleague could disagree with it
- "It depends" with no follow-through is not a stance — the panel equivalent of narrating an outline
- Write the stance in one sentence before drafting the rest

## Talking points (3–5 points, 45–90 seconds each)

Each point is a self-contained unit deployable in any order.

**Per-point template:**

```
[Bridge-in line]
[Point body — 45–90 second spoken prose block]
[Clean ending — concrete example or turn to group; no trailing off]
```

**Bridge-in patterns:**
> "That connects to something we see in [domain/data] — "  
> "Building on what [co-panelist] just said — and I think this extends further — "  
> "I want to add a piece of evidence to that point — "  
> "The question [moderator] just asked gets at something I've been thinking about — "

**Clean ending patterns:**
> "The concrete example of that is [X]."  
> "I'd be curious whether [co-panelist] has seen the same pattern."  
> "That's the data; the interpretation is still contested, and that's part of why we're here."

**Timing:** Mark each talking point with its estimated spoken duration. 60 seconds is the default; 90 seconds is the maximum before the point starts to feel like a mini-presentation.

## Moderator question responses

**Synthesis question** ("where is the field going?"):
> "[Directional claim in one sentence]. The evidence I find most compelling is [one data point or example]. The open question — and I think it's the right one to end on — is [genuine uncertainty]."

**Contrast question** ("you two seem to disagree"):
> "I think [co-panelist] and I agree that [shared premise]. Where we diverge is [specific claim]. My read of [evidence] is [position]; I suspect [co-panelist]'s read of [different evidence] leads somewhere different."

**Audience-proxy question** ("what would you tell a practitioner?"):
> "The honest advice is [1–2 concrete things]. Not [common but unhelpful advice]. The reason I'd resist that is [one sentence]."

---

## Reaction material

**Build-on:**
> "[Co-panelist name]'s point about [X] connects to something we see in [your domain]: [extension in one sentence]. So if [their claim] is right, it also implies [your extension]."

**Respectful disagreement — formula:**
> "I agree [their valid point — be specific about what you're validating]. Where I'd push back is [specific claim, not their entire argument] — in [your evidence or experience], we see [counter-evidence]. So the question for me becomes [reframe that moves the conversation forward]."

**Do not:**
- Disagree with someone's entire framework; attack a specific claim
- Use "but" to open the disagreement — it signals dismissal; use "where I'd push back" or "what I'd add"

---

## Language patterns

**Chinese panel opening (圆桌论坛):**
> 「谢谢主持人。我想直接说一个观点：[stance in one sentence]。[Evidence sentence]。[Implication for discussion]。」

Flowery throat-clearing reads as junior in Chinese panel settings. No 「很荣幸」「感谢主办方给我这个机会」at the start — begin the stance directly after thanking the moderator.

**Signaling confidence without arrogance:**
> Use "In the data I've seen..." rather than "The data shows..."  
> Use "My read is..." rather than "It's obvious that..."  
> Commit, but don't overclaim. The strongest panelists speak with authority about their evidence, not about the entire field.

---

## Time guidance

| Element | Time |
|---|---|
| Opening statement | 2–5 min (confirm allotment with moderator) |
| Each talking point | 45–90 sec |
| Moderator question response | 60–90 sec |
| Audience Q&A contribution | 30–60 sec |

**Brevity is status.** The strongest panelists speak in 45–90 second units and stop cleanly. Panelists who talk the longest are usually remembered as the weakest, not the most knowledgeable.
